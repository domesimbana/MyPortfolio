Kyoto University

________________________________________

Last summer, I studied abroad at Kyoto University through the UF in Japan Program, where I explored how culture influences innovation, social norms, and design. This experience shaped my perspective as a future global engineer focused on human-centered solutions.

As part of the program, I helped develop KeroKero, a disaster preparedness app for Kyoto residents. Using .NET MAUI, C#, and XAML, the app features evacuation routes, emergency checklists, file uploads, real-time alerts, and offline access to ensure usability during emergencies.

To guide our design, we conducted user research by surveying Kyoto students about their experiences with existing disaster preparedness apps. Their feedback helped us identify gaps in current solutions and prioritize features that would make the app more practical and intuitive for daily use.

This experience deepened my technical and cross-cultural design skills while reinforcing the importance of empathy, adaptability, and collaboration in engineering.
